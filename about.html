Built by Authors, for Authors

AuthorKit was created to solve a problem we kept seeing: talented authors spending hours wrestling with website plugins that weren't designed for books.

The result? Messy, slow sites—and author brands that didn't match the quality of the work.

We're Swati and Maulik, siblings who built AuthorKit to change that. Swati writes and publishes. Maulik designs. We both know what it's like to watch good work buried under bad tools.

Our Story

Swati spent years in roles that demanded precision under pressure—as a paramedic, then as an educator for over a decade, and later as a content writer and translator. She writes and publishes stories and poetry across English and Gujarati, exploring human emotions and relationships. Visit Swati's Journal →

But when it came to showcasing her published work online, the tools failed her. Book plugins were either bloated with features she didn't need, incompatible with her WordPress theme, or so complex they required a developer just to display a simple catalog. Her author website—the face of her work—couldn't match the care and craft she put into her writing.

Maulik has spent years as a freelance artist, designing for clients who needed their work to look professional without technical headaches. View Maulik's Portfolio → When Swati described her WordPress struggles, the problem was clear: authors shouldn't need to become developers just to showcase their books.

So we built AuthorKit. Not for everyone at first—just for authors like Swati who needed something that worked.

What We Believe

That same "make it clear, make it human, make it work" mindset that guided Swati's teaching and writing is what AuthorKit brings to WordPress.

Purpose-built for books

A book catalog should look and behave like readers expect.

Lightweight & fast

Built with vanilla PHP and JavaScript—no bloat, no dependencies.

Theme-compatible

Works with popular WordPress themes and virtually any other.

Simple to launch

Install from your WordPress dashboard in minutes—no coding required.
