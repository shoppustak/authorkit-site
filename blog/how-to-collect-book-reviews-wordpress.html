Why Collect Reviews on WordPress

Before we dive into the how, let's quickly cover the why. Collecting book reviews directly on your WordPress website offers several compelling advantages over relying solely on Amazon or Goodreads:

  • You own your social proof — Reviews stay on your site even if Amazon changes policies or your books get pulled from retailers
  • Better conversion rates — Visitors seeing reviews on your book pages are more likely to purchase
  • SEO benefits — Fresh user-generated content signals to Google that your site is active and valuable
  • Email capture opportunities — Collecting reviewer emails builds your mailing list
  • Independence from Amazon — Diversify your review sources and reduce platform dependency

Now let's explore three methods to collect reviews on your WordPress site, starting with the easiest and most comprehensive solution.

Review Moderation Best Practices

Collecting reviews is only half the equation. Effective moderation builds trust while protecting your reputation.

Approve Reviews Quickly

Readers expect to see their reviews appear within 24-48 hours. Set up email notifications and check your moderation dashboard daily. Quick approval shows you value their input.

What to Approve

Approve honest opinions, even if they're not 5-stars:

  • Detailed, thoughtful reviews (any star rating)
  • Brief but genuine reviews ("Quick read, loved it!" is fine)
  • Critical but constructive feedback (3-4 stars with specific concerns)
  • Reviews that help other readers make informed decisions

What to Reject

Don't publish reviews that are:

  • Spam or gibberish
  • Offensive or abusive language
  • Promoting competitors
  • Clearly about the wrong book
  • Duplicate submissions from the same person

Responding to Reviews

Engaging with reviewers builds community. Keep responses brief and professional:

  • Positive reviews: "Thank you for reading! So glad you enjoyed it."
  • Critical reviews: "Thanks for the feedback. I appreciate you taking the time to share your thoughts."
  • Never argue with reviewers or get defensive

Review Request Email Templates

The best reviews come from readers you actively ask. Here are proven email templates you can use to request reviews.

When to Send Review Requests

Send review requests at strategic moments:

  • 7-10 days after purchase — Gives readers time to finish the book
  • 2 weeks after book launch — Perfect for launch team members
  • In your newsletter — Ask existing readers to review their favorite book

Email Template: Simple Request

Subject: Loved [Book Title]? Quick review? Hi [Name], I hope you enjoyed [Book Title]! If you have 2 minutes, would you mind leaving a quick review on my website? [Book Page URL with Review Form] Your feedback helps other readers discover the book. Thank you! [Your Name]

Email Template: With Incentive

Subject: Review [Book Title] and enter to win! Hi [Name], Thanks for reading [Book Title]! Leave a review on my website and you'll be automatically entered to win [Prize - signed book, gift card, etc.]: [Book Page URL] Winner announced [Date]. Your review helps other readers find great books! [Your Name]

  • Make reviews scannable — Use spacing and typography for easy reading

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I get my first reviews?

Start by emailing past readers who enjoyed your book, asking your launch team, and including review requests in your book's back matter. Offer a small incentive (like entry into a giveaway) to encourage participation.

Can readers leave reviews without creating accounts?

Yes! With AuthorKit, readers can submit reviews with just their name and optional email. No registration or login required.

What if I get spam reviews?

AuthorKit's moderation system prevents spam from ever publishing. All reviews require approval before appearing on your site.

How long do reviews take to appear?

Reviews appear after you approve them from the moderation dashboard. Most authors approve within 24 hours of submission.

Can I edit reviews that readers submit?

You can fix obvious typos, but don't change the meaning or sentiment of reviews. Readers trust authentic feedback.

Do reviews help with SEO?

Yes! Reviews provide fresh user-generated content, include natural keywords, and AuthorKit automatically adds schema markup that helps reviews appear in Google search results.
